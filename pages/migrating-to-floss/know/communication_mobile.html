There is a widespread misconception that WhatsApp is secure and privacy friendly. While WhatsApp can’t see what’s inside the messages, they can see your activities, who is sending a message to whom and when - which is a large risk (read more on their policies from the links below).

And according to the WhatsApp privacy policy, the company reserves the right to record this information, known as message metadata, and share information with "others" including(but obviously not limited to) law enforcement and to respond to legal requests. If you use their service, please check their statements regularly.
They also design the privacy policy page in a way that it is hard to point to a section of their statement through links.
Details on information shared to law enforcement can be found here.
