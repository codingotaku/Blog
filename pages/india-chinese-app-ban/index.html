When I say past, I mean my young self who thought everything that's famous is cool and trendy!

These apps are a threat to all of its users and their privacy. People make a nation, and any threat to the people ARE what defines a threat for the nation.

Threat to peoples privacy risk privacy of the nation.

If I say "privacy" most people would say ,
you forget that when a company sell your info, it can also go to anyone, including their government and it could also be yours.

Usually the cover up for this massive threat is the sentence "As applicable by law" somewhere in the privacy policy and third party data collection section.
Now people are worried when a Chinese app has a similar sentence in their policy. But isn't it the same for almost every other service that you use? And how many of you have realized that?
